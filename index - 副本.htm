Biography

I am a research associate in the Software Verification and Validation Lab at University of Luxembourg, headed by Prof. Lionel Briand (IEEE/ACM Fellow). I received my Ph.D. degree from the School of Software of Dalian University of Technology in 2019, under supervision with Prof. He Jiang. I received my B.S. degree from the School of Software of Dalian University of Technology in 2012, continued with the Master of Software Engineering at School of Software from 2015. Before university, I was graduated from Harbin No. 3 High School, Harbin, China. From Octorber, 2017 to December, 2017, I worked as a research assistant with Prof. Yasutaka Kamei in the School of Information Science and Electrical Engineering at Kyushu University.

My research interests include Intelligent Software Engineering and Open-Source Software Engineering. In these researches, I analyze the crowd knowledge in the existing software engineering process and improve the development productivity or automate software tasks with machine learning techniques like representation learning and natural language analysis. The goal of my research is to analyze the current software development and improve the development productivity with artificial intelligence techniques.

Currently, I focus on improving the software engineering process in realistic settings. I analyze problems in direct collaboration with industry partners, assess the state of the art with respect to the identified problems, and devise novel, practical solutions through research. I collaborate with Huawei, BGL BNP Paribas, CSSF in Luexmoubrg to solve their problems in test alarm analysis, software input error identification, and compliance checking for financial requirements.
